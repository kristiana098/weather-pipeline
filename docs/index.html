Weather Poem

Here's a poetic comparison of the cities:

**In Sølvlys (Silver Light)**

Aalborg, Aalborg, hvor solen stråler (Aalborg, Aalborg, where the sun shines)
Et varmt bælte omkring dit hav (a warm belt around your sea)
23,7 grader, en sommerdag (23.7 degrees, a summer day)
 ingen regn, kun vinger af vind (no rain, only wings of wind)

I Grækenlands varme (In Greece's warmth)
Athens, byen af sol og sten (Athens, city of sun and stone)
15,3 grader, en behagelig temperatur (15.3 degrees, a pleasant temperature)
ingen regn, kun en blå himmel (no rain, only a blue sky)

I København, storbyens fred (In Copenhagen, the city's peace)
22,9 grader, en varm sommerdag (22.9 degrees, a warm summer day)
 ingen regn, kun en blid vind (no rain, only a gentle wind)

Men hvor er det køligste sted til i morgen? (But where is the coolest place to be tomorrow?)
Copenhagen er det absolut bedste valg (Copenhagen is the absolute best choice)
22,9, kun lidt under Aalborgs temperatur (22.9, just below Aalborg's temperature)
men Copenhagen har en blid og venlig vind (but Copenhagen has a gentle and friendly wind)

**Så gå til København i morgen**
 (So go to Copenhagen tomorrow)
og nyd den kølige sommerdag (and enjoy the cool summer day)
i København, storbyen af fred (In Copenhagen, the city of peace)

Translation:
In Silver Light
Aalborg, Aalborg, where the sun shines
A warm belt around your sea
23.7 degrees, a summer day
no rain, only wings of wind

In Greece's warmth
Athens, city of sun and stone
15.3 degrees, a pleasant temperature
no rain, only a blue sky

In Copenhagen, the city's peace
22.9 degrees, a warm summer day
no rain, only a gentle wind

But where is the coolest place to be tomorrow?
Copenhagen is the absolute best choice
22.9, just below Aalborg's temperature
but Copenhagen has a gentle and friendly wind

So go to Copenhagen tomorrow
and enjoy the cool summer day
in Copenhagen, the city of peace
