Weather Poem

**English Poem:**

Aalborg's skies weep tears of rain,
As Athens basks in a warmer reign,
While Copenhagen dances with the autumn's sigh,

Aalborg's gusts howl strong and free,
Athens' breeze whispers tenderly,
Copenhagen's winds sing a melancholy melody.

In this trio of Northern cities' sway,
Athens shines bright, come what may.
For those who seek a sun-kissed day,
Athens beckons, come what may.

**Danske Poe:**

Aalborgs himmel græder bønner til regn,
Så Athens bades i varmere årstider,
Så Copenhagen danser med efterårets sus.

Aalborgs vindstorme hvæser stærkt og frit,
Athens' brise hvisker blide,
Copenhagens vind synger en trist dags melodi.

I dette trio af nordlige byer skæve,
Athens skinner klar, hvad end være må.
For dem, der søger en solbeskinnet dag,
Athens råber til, kom hvad end være må.

(Note: 'bønner til regn' is a poetic translation of 'rain', and not a literal translation. The poem is trying to convey the idea that it's raining in Aalborg, but is using the 'græder bønner til regn' phrase to add a poetic touch.)

If you're deciding where to be tomorrow, Athens seems to be the nicest option, with clear skies and a pleasant temperature, a perfect destination for a sun-kissed day.
